Nectar corridor design — farmhouse landscape
Zone 1 — Sunbird corner (30–50 sqm)
Concentrated planting near the main veranda. Year-round flower availability. A resident pair will hold this territory and perform daily at 6:30–9:00 am and 4:30–6:00 pm.
Hibiscus hedgeRusselia cascadeCallistemon accentIxora base
Callistemon backdrop at 2 m → Hibiscus hedge at 1.2 m → Russelia cascading over a low wall → Ixora at ground level. Space plants 1–1.5 m apart. Face east or south-east for morning sun. Install a 3–5 cm deep granite water dish at 1.2 m height — sunbirds bathe after feeding. Expect first nesting in Year 2.
Zone 2 — 4-tier nectar corridor (boundary / path edge)
Layered linear planting. Different sunbird species feed at different heights, reducing territorial conflict and maximising species count on one linear run.
Erythrina canopyBauhinia midCallistemon shrubHamelia base
Erythrina at 8–10 m spacing → Bauhinia at 5–6 m → Callistemon at 2–3 m → Hamelia at 1 m. This allows Loten's (tall flowers), Purple sunbird (mid), and Purple-rumped (low shrubs) to feed simultaneously without conflict. Plant in a north–south line for maximum sun exposure across all tiers.
Zone 3 — Perch and nesting infrastructure
Sunbirds need a perching branch within 0.5–1.5 m of every nectar flower. Without perches, they will not feed — regardless of plant density.
Dead stubs retainedBamboo canes 1.2 mTrellis near shrubs
Retain all dead branch stubs — they become singing perches and future cavity sites. Push 1.2 m bamboo canes into the soil near every nectar shrub cluster. The male will use a prominent elevated perch to sing and defend territory — this perch is as important as the flowers themselves. Never remove "dead-looking" branches during Feb–May breeding season.
Zone 4 — Dedicated sunbird water feature
A 3–5 cm deep granite saucer at 1–1.5 m height near flowering shrubs. Sunbirds bathe immediately after feeding. Change water every 2 days.
3–5 cm deep onlyHeight 1–1.5 mNear shrub cover
Use a 25–35 cm diameter granite or terracotta dish. Place a flat stone inside for grip. Position within 1.5 m of the Hibiscus cluster. A solar dripper creating gentle water movement dramatically increases visits — birds are attracted to the sound of moving water. Change water every 2 days in summer to prevent mosquito breeding.
Critical rule: orient your sunbird corner east or south-east. Flowering shrubs in shade produce significantly less nectar and attract fewer birds.
